Spokane Arts Academy is a K-5 charter school opening in Fall 2027. Our classroom instruction is organized around a clear set of school rules that every student learns during the first week: Be kind, be safe, be ready to learn. These rules form the backbone of our community culture. Our teachers follow detailed lesson-planning instructions provided in our curriculum guide, which aligns with Washington State learning standards and the Commission's expectations for instructional rigor.

Our school rules emphasize restorative practices over punitive discipline. When students break a rule, teachers follow a three-step response: acknowledge the behavior, discuss the impact, and coach the student through repair. The instructions for this approach are laid out in our family handbook and reviewed in our teacher onboarding.

Instructional coaches provide teachers with weekly written instructions for standards-aligned unit planning. These instructions are not prescriptive scripts but scaffolds that help teachers maintain consistency across classrooms while still adapting lessons to student needs.

Our budget supports this model with a full-time instructional coach, curriculum stipends for teachers to refine unit-level instructions, and professional learning time built into the schedule. We believe strong classroom instructions combined with clear school rules produce a learning environment where students thrive.

We plan to serve 150 students in Year 1 at approximately 40 percent free or reduced lunch, 12 percent IEP, and 10 percent ELL. Our staffing and revenue projections match these demographics.
